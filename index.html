Circular Economy Learning Tools

A collection of interactive tools for exploring the financial, strategic, and environmental dimensions of circular economy business models. Built for students and educators — designed to build intuition, not to replace business planning.

These tools are for learning. They help you explore trade-offs, understand what drives circular business economics, and develop the critical thinking needed to evaluate real-world circular strategies. Change the inputs, watch what happens, and ask why.

How to Use These Tools
01

Pick a product

Think of a real product you know — a phone, a jacket, a chair. Estimate its production cost and selling price. Real-ish numbers make the learning stick.

02

Run the model

Enter your numbers, choose circular strategies, and calculate. Read the results — but don't stop at the numbers.

03

Read the explanations

Click "Why these numbers?" on each card. The diagnostic tells you what's driving the result and what would change it.

04

Change and explore

Adjust one parameter and watch what happens. Find the tipping points. That cycle of hypothesis → test → observe is where real understanding forms.
